From fantasy adventures to gripping thrillers and heartfelt memoirs, these books have taken the reading world by storm! Whether you’re looking for romance, mystery, or a thought-provoking story, here are five must-read books that Gen Z can’t stop talking about.





"Iron Flame" – Rebecca Yarros (Fantasy/Romance) ★★★★☆ (4.6/5) Following the events of Fourth Wing, Violet Sorrengail faces even greater challenges at Basgiath War College. With new enemies, rising dangers, and deeper betrayals, she must fight harder than ever to survive. As the war intensifies, secrets unravel, and unexpected alliances form. A thrilling mix of magic, action, and romance, Iron Flame has been a sensation among fantasy lovers.






"Love, Theoretically" – Ali Hazelwood (Romance/Contemporary) ★★★★☆ (4.3/5) Elsie Hannaway, a struggling theoretical physicist, balances her academic dreams with a secret side job—pretending to be a girlfriend for hire. But when she meets Jack, her biggest academic rival, sparks fly in ways she never expected. Filled with humor, nerdy banter, and slow-burn romance, Love, Theoretically is a fun and heartwarming read for contemporary romance fans.






"The Housemaid" – Freida McFadden (Thriller/Mystery) ★★★★☆ (4.5/5) Millie thinks she’s landed the perfect job as a live-in housemaid, but things start feeling off when she discovers the locked attic door and hears strange noises at night. As she unravels the secrets of the house, the truth is far more terrifying than she imagined. A fast-paced psychological thriller with shocking twists, The Housemaid keeps readers hooked till the last page.






"Tomorrow, and Tomorrow, and Tomorrow" – Gabrielle Zevin (Fiction/Coming-of-Age) ★★★★☆ (4.4/5) Spanning decades, this novel follows two childhood friends, Sam and Sadie, who create a groundbreaking video game together. As they navigate success, love, heartbreak, and ambition, their friendship is tested in unexpected ways. A deeply emotional story about creativity and human connection, this book has resonated strongly with Gen Z readers.






"I'm Glad My Mom Died" – Jennette McCurdy (Memoir) ★★★★★ (4.8/5) In this raw and darkly humorous memoir, iCarly star Jennette McCurdy shares her struggles with childhood fame, a toxic mother-daughter relationship, and eating disorders. Told with honesty and wit, the book sheds light on the dark side of the entertainment industry while offering a powerful story of self-discovery and healing.
