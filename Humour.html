Humour




1.The House in the Cerulean Sea – TJ Klune A heartwarming novel about Linus Baker, a caseworker who is sent to evaluate an orphanage housing magical children. As he spends time with the children and their mysterious caretaker, he learns about love, acceptance, and the true meaning of home. A whimsical and uplifting novel filled with warmth and humor. 2.Lessons in Chemistry – Bonnie Garmus Set in the 1960s, this novel follows Elizabeth Zott, a brilliant chemist who is forced to navigate a male-dominated world. When she becomes the unlikely host of a cooking show, she turns it into a platform for female empowerment, changing lives along the way. A witty, sharp, and inspiring novel that blends humor with social commentary. 3.Eleanor Oliphant Is Completely Fine – Gail Honeyman Eleanor Oliphant has a structured, isolated life—until a series of events force her to confront her past and open herself up to human connection. A humorous yet deeply moving novel about loneliness, healing, and self-discovery. 4.Finlay Donovan Is Killing It – Elle Cosimano A struggling writer is accidentally mistaken for a hitwoman and gets caught up in a real crime. A fast-paced, funny, and clever novel that blends mystery with dark humor. 5.The Rosie Project – Graeme Simsion A socially awkward professor embarks on a scientific quest to find a wife, only to meet a woman who defies all his expectations. A heartwarming and hilarious romance. 6.Remarkably Bright Creatures – Shelby Van Pelt An elderly woman finds comfort in talking to a giant octopus at the aquarium, leading to an unexpected and deeply moving friendship. A charming and emotional novel.
