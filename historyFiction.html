Historical Fiction




1.The Nightingale – Kristin Hannah Set in Nazi-occupied France during World War II, this novel follows two sisters, Vianne and Isabelle, who take different paths in the fight for survival and resistance. Vianne, a mother, is forced to shelter a Nazi officer in her home, while Isabelle joins the underground resistance, risking her life to save others. As the war unfolds, both women experience unimaginable hardships, testing their strength, love, and resilience. A powerful and deeply moving novel about courage and sacrifice. 2.The Book Thief – Markus Zusak Narrated by Death, this novel tells the story of Liesel Meminger, a young girl in Nazi Germany who finds solace in books during a time of war and destruction. As she is sent to live with foster parents, she discovers the magic of words and the power they hold. With the help of her foster father, she learns to read and begins to steal books, sharing them with others, including a Jewish man hiding in their basement. A beautifully written, heart-wrenching story that captures the resilience of the human spirit. 3.The Tattooist of Auschwitz – Heather Morris Based on a true story, this novel follows Lale Sokolov, a Jewish man who is taken to Auschwitz and becomes the camp’s tattooist, marking fellow prisoners with identification numbers. While performing this grim duty, he meets and falls in love with Gita, a fellow prisoner, and vows to protect her at all costs. Through moments of despair and hope, their love story unfolds against the backdrop of one of history’s darkest times. A deeply emotional and inspiring novel about survival, love, and resilience. 4.The Alice Network – Kate Quinn Told in dual timelines, this novel follows Charlie St. Clair in 1947 as she searches for her cousin who went missing during WWII. Her search leads her to Eve Gardiner, a former spy from WWI who was part of the real-life Alice Network, a group of female spies. As Charlie digs deeper into Eve’s past, secrets from the war unravel, exposing betrayal, courage, and an unlikely friendship. A gripping tale of espionage, survival, and redemption. 5.Beneath a Scarlet Sky – Mark Sullivan Based on the true story of Pino Lella, an Italian teenager who unwittingly becomes a spy for the Allies during WWII. Initially trying to escape the war, he ends up helping Jews escape through the Alps before being recruited to work as a driver for a high-ranking Nazi officer. Using this opportunity, he gathers intelligence for the resistance while navigating the horrors of war. A thrilling and emotional story of bravery and sacrifice. 6.Hamnet – Maggie O’Farrell A deeply evocative and poetic novel inspired by the life of William Shakespeare’s family, particularly his son, Hamnet. Set in the late 1500s, it explores the untold story of Shakespeare’s wife, Agnes, a healer with an almost mystical connection to nature. When their son falls gravely ill, the family is thrust into grief, a pain that would later inspire one of Shakespeare’s greatest plays, Hamlet. A beautifully written novel about love, loss, and the power of storytelling.
