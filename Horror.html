Horror




1.The Only Good Indians – Stephen Graham Jones Blending horror with Native American folklore, this novel follows four men who are haunted by a past mistake—an elk hunt that went terribly wrong. Years later, an entity begins stalking them, seeking revenge in a terrifying and brutal way. As they struggle with guilt, identity, and survival, the novel delivers a haunting exploration of tradition, justice, and supernatural horror. Chilling, atmospheric, and deeply unsettling. 2.Mexican Gothic – Silvia Moreno-Garcia Set in 1950s Mexico, this gothic horror novel follows Noemí, a glamorous socialite who is sent to a remote mansion to check on her cousin, who has sent alarming letters about a mysterious illness and a haunted house. As Noemí explores the mansion’s dark history, she uncovers terrifying secrets hidden within its walls. A suspenseful, eerie, and beautifully atmospheric novel with psychological horror and gothic undertones. 3.The House in the Pines – Ana Reyes A gripping psychological thriller about Maya, who witnesses her best friend mysteriously die years ago and has been haunted by that moment ever since. When another woman dies in the same way, Maya begins to unravel the secrets of her past, leading her to a secluded house where the truth may be buried. Dark, tense, and full of mind-bending twists, this novel keeps readers on edge until the final revelation. 4.Hide – Kiersten White A deadly game of hide-and-seek turns into a horror-filled nightmare when contestants realize they are being hunted. Set in an abandoned amusement park, the novel follows a group of strangers competing for a large cash prize, only to discover that something sinister lurks in the shadows. Fast-paced, eerie, and filled with psychological suspense, this novel blends horror with social commentary. 5.Home Before Dark – Riley Sager Maggie Holt returns to the infamous house that her father wrote about in a bestselling horror memoir—only to uncover whether the terrifying events he described were real or fiction. As she investigates the house’s dark history, eerie occurrences begin happening again. A spine-chilling blend of ghost story and psychological thriller that keeps readers guessing until the last page. 6.What Moves the Dead – T. Kingfisher A gothic horror retelling of Edgar Allan Poe’s The Fall of the House of Usher, filled with unsettling imagery, grotesque transformations, and a slow-building sense of dread. A deeply atmospheric and richly detailed horror novel that lingers in the mind long after finishing
